My path to data analytics runs through three very different fields. I hold a Bachelor of Commerce from Pearson Institute, where I built a foundation in accounting, finance, and management — giving me a business lens that most analysts develop only after years on the job.

After graduating I founded and ran the Vermaak Brewing Company, managing everything from recipe development to production scheduling, supply chain, and distribution. Running a business is applied analytics: you track what sells, model costs, and make data-driven decisions every day — or you fail.

Since 2024 I have been based in South Korea, teaching English while completing two Google Data Analytics certifications. I have applied Python, SQL, R, and Tableau to a diverse portfolio of real-world datasets — from HR churn prediction to NYC taxi fares — and contributed to a DataKind volunteer project on climate patterns affecting smallholder farmers. I am ready to do this full-time.

Salifort Motors — HR Churn Prediction

Problem: High employee turnover was costing Salifort Motors significant recruitment and training expenditure, with no predictive system in place.

Approach: Conducted EDA to uncover retention patterns, then built and compared a Logistic Regression model against a Random Forest classifier, using cross-validation and feature importance analysis.

Result: Random Forest achieved 98%+ accuracy, surfacing satisfaction score, monthly hours, and promotion history as the strongest churn predictors. Delivered an actionable retention strategy for the HR team.

TikTok — Claims vs. Opinions Classifier

Problem: TikTok needed to scale content moderation by automatically distinguishing factual claims from opinions in video captions — a task too large for manual review.

Approach: Feature engineering on engagement metrics and caption text, followed by Logistic Regression and Random Forest classification with cross-validation to guard against overfitting.

Result: Achieved 99% classification accuracy on the test set. Identified key engagement signals that reliably distinguish claim-based content, supporting scalable automated moderation.

Python Scikit-learn NLP Random Forest EDA

Waze — User Churn Prediction

Problem: Waze needed to identify users likely to churn before they uninstalled the app, enabling proactive retention campaigns.

Approach: Exploratory analysis of app-usage patterns, hypothesis testing to validate behavioural differences between retained and churned users, then predictive modelling with Logistic Regression and Random Forest.

Result: Surfaced high-confidence behavioural signals predicting churn risk. Packaged insights as a product team brief with prioritised intervention recommendations.
